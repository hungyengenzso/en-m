80TH ANNIVERSARY OF VIETNAM NATIONAL DAY

(September 2, 1945 - September 2, 2025)

SIGNIFICANCE OF NATIONAL DAY

September 2, 1945 is a historic milestone of the Vietnamese nation, marking the birth of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, now the Socialist Republic of Vietnam.

HISTORY OF NATIONAL DAY

On September 2, 1945, at the historic Ba Dinh Square, President Ho Chi Minh read the Declaration of Independence, giving birth to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam. This event marked the end of more than 80 years under French colonial domination and Japanese fascist invasion, opening a new era - the era of national independence and socialism.

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

National Day September 2 is not only a great holiday of the nation but also a symbol of the aspiration for independence and freedom, expressing the indomitable will and spirit of solidarity of the entire Vietnamese nation. This is an occasion for us to recall the heroic tradition of our forefathers and educate the younger generation about patriotism and national pride.

80-YEAR JOURNEY

Important historical milestones of the country from independence to present

September 2, 1945

President Ho Chi Minh reads the Declaration of Independence, establishing the Democratic Republic of Vietnam

1946

The first General Election to elect the National Assembly of Vietnam

1954

Dien Bien Phu Victory, ending the resistance war against the French

1975

Liberation of the South, reunification of the country

1986

The country enters the Đổi Mới (Renovation) period

1995

Vietnam joins ASEAN and normalizes relations with the United States

2007

Vietnam becomes the 150th member of the World Trade Organization (WTO)

2020-2025

Vietnam affirms its position internationally, with strong economic and social development

Vietnamese Youth and the Spirit of National Day

Every September, streets are covered with red flags and yellow stars, and Vietnamese people joyfully celebrate National Day on September 2 - the day when President Ho Chi Minh read the Declaration of Independence at the historic Ba Dinh Square, giving birth to the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, now the Socialist Republic of Vietnam. This is not only a great national holiday but also an opportunity for today's younger generation to feel more proud and more conscious of their responsibility to build and protect the Fatherland.

For the younger generation, National Day September 2 not only recalls the heroic history of our forefathers but also ignites the flame of enthusiasm to study, create, and contribute to the country. The spirit of independence from the past is now illuminated in the aspiration to conquer knowledge, in the spirit of commitment to the community, and in the will to bring Vietnam to new heights.

Joining the atmosphere of the whole country, we - the young people - feel a special energy. It is the pride of living in an independent, free and developing country. Our generation has never experienced the years of bombs and bullets, never had to shed blood to gain independence. But we always remember the gratitude of the previous generations who sacrificed to build a peaceful, unified Vietnam.

The spirit of September 2 for Generation Z is not only expressed in decorating houses, joining the crowd going on holiday, or wearing the red and yellow flag shirt. That spirit also lies in responsibility, in the pioneering spirit, in the desire to contribute.

In the context of global integration, Vietnamese youth are facing many opportunities and challenges. We can easily access human knowledge, cultural exchange, and expand our vision. However, we also need to be alert to maintain our identity and not lose our core values. The spirit of independence and self-reliance of our nation has been tempered through thousands of years of history. That is the guiding principle for us to move forward firmly.

The youth of today - the generation of the integration period - are the core force, the trust and expectation of the Fatherland. Each research project, each startup idea, each charitable action... is proof of patriotism expressed through practical actions.

In anticipation of National Day September 2, Vietnamese youth are proud to hold their heads high, promising to live beautifully, live usefully and carry in their hearts the flame of love for the country to contribute to building a rich, strong, civilized and happy Vietnam.

National Day - the festival of the country. National Day - the day when young people remind each other to live worthy of the heroic tradition of the nation.
