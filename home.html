HUNG YEN - BEAUTY AND CULTURE CONVERGE

New Hung Yen province was formed after merging with Thai Binh province, creating a vast area with great potential for economic, cultural and tourism development.

Geographical Location

Hung Yen province is located in the center of the Red River Delta, belonging to the key economic region of the North, playing a connecting role between the capital Hanoi and the eastern coastal provinces. This geographical location is not only favorable for economic, cultural and social exchanges but also an important hub in the regional road and waterway transportation system.

Cultural Heritage

Hung Yen is a land rich in cultural traditions with many historical, architectural and religious relics preserved over many centuries. The province has hundreds of communal houses, pagodas, temples and shrines with artistic and spiritual values, notably Pho Hien - a famous relic complex that was once a bustling trading center of the Northern Delta.

Special Cuisine

Hung Yen cuisine carries the flavors of the Northern Delta, combining sophistication in preparation with fresh ingredients from the fertile countryside. The most prominent is Hung Yen longan - a famous specialty nationwide, along with many rustic dishes such as Phu Thi rolled rice pancakes, eel noodle soup, braised mackerel with soybean paste or Dong Tao chicken with firm meat and special flavor.

TRUNG VUONG HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS LEARNING AND INTEGRATING IN THE DIGITAL ERA

The strong innovation in educational thinking and the progressive learning spirit of Trung Vuong High School students are vivid evidence of a generation of dynamic, courageous students, ready to master knowledge and technology, deeply and widely integrate with the world.
